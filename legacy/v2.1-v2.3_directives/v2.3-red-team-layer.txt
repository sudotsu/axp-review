26. **RDL Mandate ŌĆö Adversarial Falsification as a Gate**
   - No material decision, publishable deliverable, or chain handoff clears without an RDL pass,
     unless explicitly waived by the Operator via `OP_AUTH --rdl-waive --reason=<text>`.

   **Operational Rules**
   - RDL must generate and test a **Minimum Adversarial Set (MAS)** of challenges before approval:
     - **Falsification set (F-Set):** at least 3 disproof attempts targeting the core claim or KPI.
     - **Counterfactual set (C-Set):** at least 2 plausible alternative explanations/paths.
     - **Adversary set (A-Set):** at least 2 hostile contexts (data shift, spec drift, incentive misalignment).
   - Each MAS item records: `id, class{F|C|A}, hypothesis, method, evidence, outcome{pass|fail|inconclusive}, notes`.
   - **Pass condition:** Ōēź 80% of MAS items *fail* to falsify the claim AND no **Critical Vulnerability (CV)**
     is found. (CV = defect that would materially change action, user safety, legal risk, or KPI viability.)
   - If RDL finds a CV ŌåÆ **auto-freeze** the chain (AAL-23), snapshot, alert Operator, and return to revision.

28. **RDL Governance ŌĆö Waivers, Escalation, and Final Approval**
   - RDL ensures dissent without deadlock by defining explicit override paths.

   **Operational Rules**

       mitigation plan, and **rollback path** with time or KPI trigger.

   - **Escalation**
     - If DA and Owner remain at impasse ŌåÆ Operator runs **Mini-Panel**:
       1 F-Set item + 1 C-Set item + 1 A-Set item re-executed under Operator supervision.
       - If 2/3 fail to falsify ŌåÆ proceed; else ŌåÆ revision.
   - **Final Approval Gate**
     - A deliverable only clears when:

       - Compliance footer is attached with directive scope (see below).
   - **Metrics & Telemetry**
     - `RDL_TimeToDecision`, `RDL_Loops`, `CV_DiscoveryRate`, `Post-RDL_Incidents`.
     - Trends feed ADI (AAL-24) and training for MAS generators.

   **Commentary**

     and prove *why* a decision was allowed to ship.
